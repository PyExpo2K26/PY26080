
## Intelligent Pipeline Inspection System With Gas Sensing and Automated Defect Detection

##  Project Description
Pipeline systems used for transporting gas, oil, and other hazardous materials often develop problems such as cracks, corrosion, and leakage due to aging, environmental conditions, and mechanical stress. These defects can cause serious safety risks, environmental damage, and financial losses if they are not detected early. Traditional inspection methods mainly depend on manual checking, which is time-consuming, costly, and sometimes dangerous for workers because pipelines may be located in confined or hazardous environments. Another major issue is gas leakage, which can lead to explosions, health hazards, and accidents if it remains unnoticed for a long time. Many existing systems also lack continuous real-time monitoring, making it difficult to identify pipeline defects immediately. Therefore, there is a need for an intelligent pipeline inspection system with gas sensing and automated defect detection to continuously monitor pipeline conditions and improve safety, reliability, and efficiency

##  Objectives

--Detect gas leaks in pipelines using gas sensing technology
--Identify pipeline defects such as cracks, corrosion, and blockages automatically
--Provide real-time monitoring of pipeline conditions
--Improve safety by giving early warning of hazardous gas leakage

##  How the System Works
--Gas sensors placed in or near the pipeline continuously monitor the surrounding environment to detect the presence of harmful gases or leaks.
--Additional sensors or inspection mechanisms check the physical condition of the pipeline to identify defects such as cracks, corrosion, or blockages.
--The Arduino microcontroller collects data from all the sensors and processes the information to determine whether the pipeline condition is normal or abnormal.
--If the system detects gas leakage or structural defects, it triggers an alert and sends the data through a wireless communication module (such as RF, Wi-Fi, or Bluetooth).
--The received information is displayed on a monitoring device or computer so that operators can quickly identify the problem and take necessary maintenance actions.
